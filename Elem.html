ELEMENTARY

Key Stage Standards:

For Key Stage 1 (Grade 3), learners are expected to demonstrate understanding and appreciation of fundamental mathematical concepts such as whole numbers up to 10,000, the four basic operations (including money), ordinal numbers up to the 100th, and the basic idea of fractions. They should also be able to measure time, length, mass, capacity, and the area of squares and rectangles; identify and describe 2-D and 3-D shapes; recognize lines, symmetry, and tessellation; create and interpret continuous or repeating patterns and number sentences; and collect, organize, and represent data in tables, pictographs, and bar graphs, including identifying possible outcomes. By the end of Key Stage 2 (Grades 4 to 6), learners are expected to build on these skills, showing competence in whole numbers, number theory, fractions, decimals, ratio and proportion, percent, and integers. They should be able to measure time, speed, perimeter, circumference, and area of plane figures; determine the volume and surface area of solid figures; read temperatures and meter readings; and understand geometric concepts such as parallel and perpendicular lines, angles, triangles, quadrilaterals, polygons, circles, and solid figures. They are also expected to work with patterns, sequences, simple equations, and a variety of graphs such as bar, line, and pie graphs, as well as perform simple experiments and calculate experimental probabilities. These standards emphasize the application of skills in real-life contexts, the use of appropriate technology, and the development of problem-solving, reasoning, and communication skills.

Grade 3

    Numbers & Operations:
  • Place value up to thousands
  • Addition and subtraction of multi-digit numbers
  • Basic multiplication and division facts (up to 10 x 10)
    Fractions:
  • Understanding halves, thirds, fourths, etc.
    Measurement:
  • Length, mass, and capacity using standard units
    Geometry:
  • Identifying basic shapes and their properties
    Data Handling:
  • Reading and creating simple bar graphs and pictographs

Grade 4

    Numbers & Operations:
  • Multiplication and division of larger numbers
  • Introduction to factors and multiples
  • Basic multiplication and division facts (up to 10 x 10)
    Fractions & Decimals:
  • Comparing, ordering, and simple operations with fractions
  • Introduction to decimals (tenths, hundredths)
    Measurement:
  • Perimeter and area of simple shapes
    Geometry:
  • Lines, angles, and symmetry
    Data Handling:
  • Interpreting line plots and bar graphs

Grade 5

    Numbers & Operations:
  • Larger whole numbers and operations
  • Order of operations (BODMAS)
    Fractions & Decimals:
  • Addition, subtraction, and multiplication of fractions/decimals
    Measurement:
  • Volume and surface area of cubes and rectangular prisms
    Geometry:
  • Classifying triangles and quadrilaterals
    Data Handling:
  • Mean, median, and mode basics

Grade 6

    Numbers & Operations:
  • Integers (positive and negative numbers)
  • Ratios and proportions
    Fractions, Decimals & Percents:
  • Conversions between them
  • Percentage problems (discounts, profit/loss)
    Algebra Basics:
  • Simple expressions and one-step equations
    Geometry:
  • Circles, polygons, and coordinate plane basicss
    Statistics:
  • Interpreting complex bar graphs and pie charts
